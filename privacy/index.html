Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 14, 2026

This policy explains how lirova (“we,” “us”) handles information across two things: this website (lirova.github.io) and the self-hosted lirova applications you may run (including any application that connects to your Google account). The short version: we collect as little as possible, and for the self-hosted apps we never receive a copy of your data at all.

1. The website

This site is a static page. It sets no cookies, runs no analytics, and uses no third-party trackers or advertising.

If you use the contact form, you send us your name, email, optional company, and your message. That information is delivered to a private inbox we control and is used for one purpose: to reply to you. It is not sold, rented, or shared with anyone, and we keep it only as long as needed to handle your enquiry.

2. The self-hosted applications

Our applications are self-hosted: they run on your machines, in your environment, using your own credentials and API keys. Because of this architecture:

  • We do not receive, store, or transmit your data. Your content stays on infrastructure you control.
  • If an application connects to your Google account (for example, to read Gmail or Calendar), it requests access on your device and uses that access locally to provide the feature you asked for.
  • OAuth tokens and any cached data are stored locally on your own machine, encrypted at rest (AES-256-GCM), never on our servers.
  • We never use your Google data for advertising, never sell it, and never transfer it to third parties.

3. Google API Services — Limited Use

lirova’s use and transfer of information received from Google APIs to any other app will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. Specifically, data obtained through Google APIs is used only to provide or improve the user-facing features you have enabled, is processed locally in your own environment, is not transferred to others except as necessary to provide those features, and is not used for advertising or sold to anyone.

4. Data sharing

We do not share your personal information with third parties. The self-hosted model means there is no central copy of your data for anyone to access, leak, or sell.

5. Security

Website enquiries are delivered over encrypted (HTTPS) connections to a private inbox. In the self-hosted applications, sensitive data is encrypted at rest on your own device. No system is perfectly secure, but because your data does not leave your environment, the exposure surface is minimal by design.

6. Your rights

You can ask us to access or delete any information you sent us via the contact form. For the self-hosted applications, you are in full control of your own data and can delete it directly. To make a request, email .

7. Children

Our services are intended for businesses and professionals and are not directed to children under 16. We do not knowingly collect their data.

8. Changes

We may update this policy as the services evolve. Material changes will be reflected by the “last updated” date above.
